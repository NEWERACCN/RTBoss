RTBoss ¬∑ Workforce Architecture

Digital Workforce

The complete workforce model ‚Äî who executes work, how they're composed, and what powers them. Every Mini-OS is staffed by three worker types and structured around three building blocks. Understanding the difference between Systems, Genies, and Streams is foundational to building correctly.

The Three Worker Types ‚Äî Who Executes Work

Work is classified by who or what executes it. Classification determines routing, automation potential, and SLA. It is not static ‚Äî work matures from Human ‚Üí Hybrid ‚Üí Digital as the Agentic Factory is built out.

ü§ñ

Digital Worker

Fully autonomous. Agent handles end-to-end with no human in the loop. Deterministic, high-confidence, in-policy.

Execution Pattern

Fact arrives ‚Üí Genie executes OR Stream runs ‚Üí Outcome logged to Digital Twin ‚Üí No human involved

Examples
  • Password reset (Genie auto-executes)
  • Patch deployment (Stream orchestrates)
  • Invoice under auto-approval threshold
  • Scheduled report generation
AutonomousNo human loopHigh volume
ü§ù

Hybrid Worker

Agent does the heavy lifting, human provides judgment or approval at the gate. True collaboration between digital and human.

Execution Pattern

Fact arrives ‚Üí Genie/Stream prepares work ‚Üí Surfaces decision to human ‚Üí Human decides ‚Üí Agent completes ‚Üí Outcome logged

Examples
  • Expense over threshold (agent validates, human approves)
  • Complex troubleshooting (agent diagnoses, human decides)
  • Contract review (agent flags risks, human confirms)
  • Ambiguous classification (agent scores, human chooses)
Agent + HumanDecision gateQuality control
üë§

Human Worker

Human drives execution end-to-end. Requires expertise, creativity, relationships, or strategic judgment that cannot be systematised.

Execution Pattern

Fact arrives ‚Üí Assigned to human ‚Üí Human executes (may invoke Genies for research or drafting support) ‚Üí Outcome logged

Examples
  • Client escalation requiring empathy
  • Architectural design decisions
  • Sales negotiation
  • Strategic planning
  • Novel problem-solving
Human-ledAgent supportHigh judgment

Key principle: Classification determines execution path, not arrival pattern. An urgent email might be Digital Worker (password reset) or Human Worker (CEO escalation). Work type is about who executes, not when it arrived or how urgent it is.

The Three Building Blocks ‚Äî How Each Mini-OS Is Composed

Every Mini-OS is built from exactly three components ‚Äî Systems, Genies, and Streams. These are not interchangeable. Each plays a distinct architectural role. Getting the distinction right is the difference between a well-structured Mini-OS and a pile of agents with no coherence.

Systems
Governing Models & Frameworks
The constitution of a Mini-OS domain

Systems are the intelligence frameworks that define how a domain operates ‚Äî the rules, methodologies, and operating models that all Genies and Streams must follow. They are not agents. They do not execute tasks. They govern.

Finance OS Examples
Profit First System ‚Äî governs how money flows, budget allocation rules, account structures
Forecasting System ‚Äî defines methodology, inputs, confidence intervals
Reporting System ‚Äî standards for what gets reported, format, cadence
RulesFrameworksNot agents
Genies
Single-Step Specialist Agents
The tactical workers of a Mini-OS

Genies are specialist agents that execute discrete, bounded tasks. They operate within the rules defined by Systems ‚Äî they don't invent the rules, they apply them. Each Genie does one thing well. Invoked on demand, either by a Stream or directly by a worker.

Finance OS Examples
Budget Builder Genie ‚Äî follows Profit First System rules to generate a budget
Tax Reporting Genie ‚Äî uses the financial model to produce compliant reports
End of Month Genie ‚Äî executes the close process per Reporting System standards
AgentsDiscrete tasksOn-demand
Streams
End-to-End Business Processes
The operational heartbeat of a Mini-OS

Streams are ongoing operational workflows ‚Äî not one-off outputs, but recurring processes with a defined start, sequence of steps, and end state. A Stream may invoke multiple Genies and follow System rules throughout. They are the engine that keeps a Mini-OS running.

Finance OS Examples
File Monthly Taxes (AU) Stream ‚Äî recurring process: gather ‚Üí calculate ‚Üí validate ‚Üí lodge
Annual Reports Stream ‚Äî end-to-end: data consolidation ‚Üí drafting ‚Üí review ‚Üí publish
EMS Order Management Stream ‚Äî order received ‚Üí validated ‚Üí fulfilled ‚Üí delivered
Workflows Multi-step Recurring
Systems ‚Üí the constitution (rules & frameworks Genies/Streams must follow) Genies ‚Üí discrete specialist agents (execute bounded tasks within System rules) Streams ‚Üí end-to-end business processes (invoke Genies, follow Systems, run continuously)

RTBoss ¬∑ Intelligence Layer ¬∑ Real-Time Business Intelligence

RTBI ‚Äî Real-Time Business Intelligence

RTBI is not a reporting tool bolted onto operations ‚Äî it is what operations look like when they run correctly on RTBoss. Two foundational components (Unified Data and the Digital Twin) make three intelligence components possible (Planning, Analytics, Decisions). You cannot have the top without the bottom.

Foundation: Unified Data ¬∑ Digital Twin ‚Üí these make possible ‚Üí Intelligence: Planning ¬∑ Analytics ¬∑ Decisions Every metric, forecast, and recommendation is a direct expression of live Fact data ‚Äî not a report compiled after the fact.
Foundation Layer ‚Äî The Infrastructure Intelligence Runs On

These two components are not intelligence outputs ‚Äî they are the substrate that makes intelligence possible. Unified Data ensures every component works from the same live numbers. The Digital Twin ensures every decision has a complete, replayable history behind it. Both must exist before Planning, Analytics, or Decisions mean anything.

üóÑÔ∏è
Unified Data
Single source of truth ¬∑ No ETL ¬∑ Live

One data layer across all Mini-OS instances. Every Fact from every domain ‚Äî finance, operations, logistics, HR ‚Äî flows into a single governed, real-time fabric. There is no reconciliation process because there is no second copy. One number, updated the moment the Fact occurred.

Without this, every downstream intelligence component is working from different versions of reality. Unified Data is the reason RTBI can be trusted.

This means two things that traditional systems cannot do: replay any moment in business history to understand exactly why an outcome occurred, and test any proposed change against real historical data before it touches production. Zero production risk on every process change.

Event Stream vs Fact Stream ‚Äî Critical Distinction

Not Every Event Is a Fact

Just because something happened doesn't mean it needs to become a Fact in the RTBoss loop. Without this filter, the Fact stream would be overwhelmed with noise ‚Äî millions of routine events that require no action. We use a two-tier architecture to separate signal from noise.

üìä Event Stream (Tier 1)

Everything that happens. High volume, mostly routine. Stays in source systems (Zero Touch AI, UniFi, Azure Monitor, access control databases).

Examples (NOT Facts)
  • Server receives 10,000 log entries/minute
  • Access control logs 500 badge swipes/day
  • Network heartbeat every 30 seconds
  • Temperature sensor reading every 5 minutes (within range)
  • Successful backup completion (expected)
High volume Routine No action needed

‚ö° Fact Stream (Tier 2)

Events that require attention. Low volume, high signal. Enters RTBoss loop, gets Dimensions, routed to Workers.

Examples (ARE Facts)
  • Server CPU >90% for 5 minutes ‚Üí resource_threshold_exceeded
  • Badge denied 3 times ‚Üí access_denied_repeated
  • Device heartbeat missed 10min ‚Üí device_offline
  • Temp outside range ‚Üí environmental_alert
  • Invoice received ‚Üí invoice_received
Low volume Actionable Requires decision

The Promotion Rule: Event ‚Üí Fact

An Event becomes a Fact when it meets ANY of these criteria:

  • Threshold crossed ‚Äî metric outside acceptable range (CPU >90%, disk <10%, response time >2s)
  • Pattern detected ‚Äî repeated failures, anomaly, unusual sequence (3 failed logins, traffic spike)
  • State change ‚Äî transition from one state to another (online ‚Üí offline, pending ‚Üí approved)
  • Business event ‚Äî transactional event requiring processing (invoice received, order placed, payment due)
  • Human input ‚Äî any human-generated communication or request (email, call, form, chat, meeting action)
‚óé
Anti-pattern: Do NOT promote routine operational events to Facts. A successful login is not a Fact. A normal temperature reading is not a Fact. A heartbeat ping is not a Fact. These stay in their source systems for compliance, forensics, and historical analysis ‚Äî but they don't enter the RTBoss loop.
