Nonconformance & Corrective Action

Every deviation from a defined requirement is captured as a Non-Conformance Report (NCR). The discipline of raising, investigating, correcting, and verifying NCRs is what separates a system that learns from one that accumulates technical debt.

â¬¡
ISO 9001 Cl.10.2 and ISO 27001 Cl.10.1 are explicit: React to the nonconformity. Deal with consequences. Determine if similar nonconformities exist. Implement corrective action. Review effectiveness. Retain documented information as evidence. An NCR without a verified corrective action is an open audit finding.
What Triggers an NCR

Agent Failure

A Genie produces an incorrect, incomplete, or unsafe output. Confidence score below threshold. Output causes a downstream process failure.

Process Deviation

A defined RTBoss process was not followed. Sprint cycle skipped. Go-live gate not completed. Document classification not applied.

Client Complaint

A client raises a formal concern about RTBoss outputs, service delivery, or SLA performance. Any NPS below target. Outcome Bonus disputed.

Security Event

Any event that may have compromised information confidentiality, integrity, or availability. Unauthorised access attempt. Data classification breach.

Audit Finding

An internal or external audit finds a gap. An observation not corrected becomes a minor NC. An unaddressed minor NC becomes a major NC.

Proactive Identification

Team member identifies a process that could fail. "We should raise an NCR" is a cultural indicator of a mature QMS â€” it means the team owns quality.

NCR Lifecycle â€” 5-Why Root Cause Method
Step 1 Â· Immediate

Raise the NCR

Anyone on the NEIT team can raise an NCR. Create ENT-006 record in Dataverse (NCR-YYYY-nnn). Set severity, describe the nonconformance, assign owner. Notify Tech Lead for MAJOR/CRITICAL.

Step 2 Â· 24h (CRITICAL), 5 days (MAJOR), 10 days (MINOR)

Immediate Containment

Isolate the failing component. Notify affected parties. Document the containment action in immediate_action field. For security incidents: follow NEIT-POL-INC-001.

Step 3 Â· Root Cause Analysis

5-Why Investigation

Ask "Why?" five times. The first answer is usually a symptom; the fifth is usually a process or design failure. Document the 5-Why chain in root_cause. Involve the person closest to the failure.

Step 4 Â· Corrective Action

Fix the Root Cause, Not the Symptom

Define what permanently prevents recurrence. Update the relevant Genie, process, or document. Create a Changelog entry. Update the test suite to catch this class of failure in future.

Step 5 Â· 30 to 90 days after correction

Verify the Fix Worked

Verify the corrective action prevented recurrence. Check output quality, review logs. Document in effectiveness_check. Only then set status to VERIFIED and CLOSED. An NCR not verified remains open for ISO purposes.

Step 6 Â· Playbook Update

Prevent It System-Wide

For MAJOR or CRITICAL NCRs: does this indicate a playbook gap? Document the playbook_update field with the section and version changed. This is how RTBoss learns.

Lesson Capture & Continual Improvement

ISO 9001 Cl.10.3 requires continual improvement of the QMS. Lessons are captured at every sprint review, every maturation review, and every NCR closure. The system must demonstrably improve over time â€” not just operate.

â¬¡
Continual improvement is mandatory under ISO 9001 Cl.10.3. The standard requires that the organisation continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the QMS. "Things are working fine" is not evidence of continual improvement â€” it must be demonstrated through logged actions and their verified outcomes.
Lesson Capture Triggers

Sprint Review (Every 2 Weeks)

At the end of every sprint, 10 minutes is dedicated to "what worked, what didn't, what would we do differently." Captured as 2â€“3 bullets in the sprint plan. Patterns across 3+ sprints become formal improvement proposals.

Maturation Review (30/90/180 days)

Structured client reviews capture lessons at scale. How did the agent perform vs expectation? What surprised us? These feed the Section 10 Factory Library agent improvement backlog.

NCR Closure

Every closed MAJOR or CRITICAL NCR generates at minimum one lesson â€” documented before the NCR is set to VERIFIED. If the same lesson appears in 3+ NCRs, it must become a playbook update.

New Client Onboarding

Every new client brings new context and edge cases. Captured during onboarding and at end of first sprint. Often reveals QMS template or agent logic gaps not visible in existing clients.

Internal Audit Programme

Both ISO standards mandate planned internal audits at defined intervals. The programme must consider the importance of processes and the results of previous audits. Auditors must be objective and impartial â€” they cannot audit their own work.

â¬¡

Management Review

Top management must review the QMS and ISMS at planned intervals to ensure continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness. This is a leadership obligation â€” not a delegatable task. Outputs must include decisions and actions.

â¬¡
Both ISO standards (9001 Cl.9.3 and 27001 Cl.9.3) require defined inputs and outputs. A management review without documented inputs and outputs is not a compliant review â€” it is just a meeting. The minutes must capture what was reviewed, what was decided, and who is accountable for what action by when.

Quarterly â€” minimum 4 per year. Additional reviews triggered by: CRITICAL NCR, significant security incident, major business change, or pre-certification audit preparation.

Typically 60â€“90 minutes â€” adequate to genuinely discuss each input item. If items are deferred, schedule a follow-up within 2 weeks. Not a tick-box exercise.
