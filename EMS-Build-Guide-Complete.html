Agentic RTBoss Delivery

Engagement Model

Every RTBoss engagement follows a six-stage continuous delivery model. We deploy infrastructure and QMS first, then build agents iteratively based on client-defined outcomes. No fixed-scope SOWs — we contract on business results and deliver whatever agents achieve them.

?
The agentic shift: Traditional delivery = "build these 12 specified things." Agentic delivery = "achieve these 3 business outcomes — we'll build whatever agents are needed, learn what works, and deploy continuously." The client buys outcomes. We deliver agents. The system learns and compounds.

Key Principles
01
Outcomes, not features: Clients don't buy "12 Genies and 3 Streams" — they buy measurable business results. We map outcomes to agents, not the other way around. The build queue emerges from what the business needs to achieve, not what we think they should have.
02
QMS before agents: The QMS (identity, standards, ontology, dictionary) must exist before any agent is built. Agents with no context produce generic outputs. Agents grounded in the client's QMS sound like they belong to that business. QMS is the foundation — agents are the tools.
03
Continuous delivery, not big bang: Agents are built in 2-week sprints. Each sprint: design 1 agent ? build ? test ? deploy. The system learns incrementally. What works compounds. What doesn't gets refined or replaced. No "go-live day" — the system is always live and always improving.
04
Workflow as proof: The first Growth or Fulfillment workflow is the proof point — not the end state. When 3+ agents are live and one end-to-end workflow runs with <15% human intervention, the model is proven. Then we expand to the next workflow. Workflows compound on agents.

Stage 01

Discovery

Discovery in the agentic model is outcome-focused, not system-focused. We're not mapping which Mini-OS to deploy — we're identifying business problems and defining measurable outcomes. The output is an Outcome Map, not a technical spec.

?
Discovery mindset shift: Don't ask "what systems do you use?" Ask "what keeps you up at night?" Don't ask "how many invoices per month?" Ask "what would change if invoice processing took 2 days instead of 2 weeks?" Listen for pain, frustration, and aspiration — these are the outcomes agents will deliver.
Discovery Sessions
SESSION 1
Leadership Interview — Outcomes & Strategy

Duration: 90 minutes
Attendees: Client sponsor, CSM, Technical Lead (observer)

The goal is to understand what success looks like and what's blocking it. Questions to ask:

  • Big picture: What does this business look like in 3 years if everything goes right? What's the single biggest thing standing between you and that vision?
  • Cost & efficiency: Where do you feel like you're spending money you shouldn't have to? What's your biggest uncontrolled cost?
  • Revenue & growth: What would it take to double revenue without doubling headcount? Where does the sales process slow down or leak?
  • People & capacity: If you could give your team back 10 hours a week, what would they do with it? What do they complain about?
  • Risk & compliance: What keeps you up at night from a legal, financial, or operational risk perspective?

Output: 3–5 high-level business outcomes the client cares about. Write them in their language, not technical language.

SESSION 2
Operations Interview — Problems & Workflows

Duration: 90 minutes
Attendees: Operations lead, CSM, Technical Lead

The goal is to understand daily reality — where the friction actually is. Questions to ask:

  • Daily operations: Walk me through your Monday morning. What are the first five things you do? What lands in your inbox that you wish didn't?
  • Repetitive work: What's the most repetitive task your team does? How long does it take per week? What would happen if it was automated?
  • Escalations & failures: When something goes wrong — a missed deadline, a client complaint, a compliance issue — how do you find out? How long does it take to fix?
  • Information gaps: Where does critical information live that it shouldn't? (Spreadsheets, email threads, people's heads) What breaks if a key person is sick for a week?
  • Growth blockers: If you got 50% more customers tomorrow, what would break first? What doesn't scale?

Output: List of specific problems mapped to workflows (e.g., "Invoice processing takes 10 days, causes cash flow issues")

Outcome Definition Workshop
WORKSHOP
Defining Measurable Outcomes

Duration: 2 hours
Attendees: Sponsor, Ops Lead, CSM, Technical Lead

Take the outputs from Sessions 1 and 2 and turn them into 3–5 measurable outcomes. Use this structure:

Outcome format: [Action] + [Metric] + [Timeframe] + [Current vs Target]

Examples:

  • Cost reduction: "Reduce invoice processing cost by 40% within 90 days" (Current: $12k/month ? Target: $7.2k/month)
  • Time saved: "Free up 20 hours per week of Finance team time within 60 days" (Current: 60 hrs/week manual work ? Target: 40 hrs/week)
  • Revenue growth: "Increase lead-to-close rate by 25% within 120 days" (Current: 8% ? Target: 10%)
  • Compliance: "Achieve 100% on-time regulatory reporting within 30 days" (Current: 70% on-time ? Target: 100%)
  • Customer satisfaction: "Reduce support ticket resolution time by 50% within 90 days" (Current: 48 hrs ? Target: 24 hrs)

Outcomes must be:

  • Measurable: Can be tracked with a number (%, hours, dollars, count)
  • Attributable: The system can prove the outcome was achieved (before/after baseline)
  • Valuable: The client would pay a bonus to achieve it
  • Timebound: Has a target date (30/60/90/120 days typical)

Output: Outcome Map — the 3–5 prioritized outcomes that will form the basis of the Outcome Contract

Discovery Report Structure
REPORT
Producing the Discovery Report

The Discovery Report is the gate output for Stage 01. Delivered within 3 business days of the Outcome Workshop. Use the QMS template. Contains:

  • Executive Summary — 1 page. Current state in client's language. Top 3 pain points. The opportunity (what changes if outcomes are achieved).
  • Outcome Map — The 3–5 defined outcomes with current state, target state, timeframe, and measurement method.
  • Problem Inventory — List of specific operational problems identified, grouped by workflow (e.g., Finance, Sales, Operations)
  • Workflow Candidates — Which Growth or Fulfillment workflows are most relevant to the client based on their problems (pick 2–3 for next stage consideration)
  • Preliminary Agent Hypothesis — High-level guess at which agent types might deliver the outcomes (Genies, Advisors, Streams). Not a commitment — just directional.
  • Risks & Dependencies — What could prevent outcome achievement (data quality, change resistance, integration complexity)

Deliver to sponsor, schedule 30-min review call, get sign-off before proceeding to Outcome Mapping.

Stage 02

Outcome Mapping & Contract

Turn the Outcome Map into an Outcome Contract — the commercial agreement that defines what business results the client will achieve and what they pay when those results are delivered. This replaces the traditional fixed-scope SOW.

From Outcomes to Agents
MAPPING
Outcome ? Agent Mapping Process

For each outcome in the Outcome Map, define which agent types are needed to deliver it. Use this logic:

  • If the outcome requires classification or routing decisions ? Genies (discrete specialist agents)
  • If the outcome requires strategic guidance or recommendations ? Advisors (senior-scope AI with broader domain knowledge)
  • If the outcome requires end-to-end process automation ? Streams (multi-Genie workflows)
  • If the outcome requires real-time monitoring and alerts ? Systems (governing frameworks that watch fact streams)

Pilot Workflow Selection
PILOT
Choose the First Workflow to Prove

From the 2–3 workflow candidates identified in Discovery, select ONE to be the pilot. Use these criteria:

  • Highest outcome impact: Which workflow, if automated, delivers the most measurable outcome value?
  • Clearest success metric: Can we prove it worked with data we can access?
  • Fewest dependencies: Which workflow requires the least integration with legacy systems or manual processes we don't control?
  • Sponsor visibility: Which workflow does the client sponsor see and care about most?

The pilot workflow will be activated in Stage 06 once the required agents are built. It is the proof point that validates the entire engagement.

Stage 03

Infrastructure Setup

Infrastructure must be live before QMS or agents. This stage is identical across all pillars and all clients — Security baseline (week 1), Workspace + Dataverse (week 2). No shortcuts. No exceptions.

?
Infrastructure is pillar-agnostic: Whether the client is deploying a Growth Engine or a Fulfillment Engine, the foundation is identical. Security, M365, Dataverse, Power Platform — these are prerequisites for any RTBoss deployment. Build them once, build them correctly, never revisit.
Week 1 · Security Baseline
W1
Security & Identity Foundation

Reference: Section 05 · Tab 08 · Security & Compliance. Complete in this exact order:

  • Entra ID: Conditional access policies, MFA for all users, Privileged Identity Management for admin roles
  • Intune: Device enrollment, compliance policies, app protection. All RTBoss-accessing devices must be compliant.
  • Defender: Defender for Business or E3/E5. Enable attack surface reduction, endpoint detection.
  • Purview: Data classification labels, DLP policies. Required before any business data enters Dataverse.
  • Admin accounts: Break-glass accounts created, documented, stored in vault. Never use global admin for routine work.

Do not proceed to Week 2 until all items verified and signed off.

Stage 04

QMS Setup

The QMS (Quality Management System) is the identity and standards layer that grounds all agents in the client's context. It must be instantiated BEFORE any agents are built. Without QMS, agents are generic. With QMS, agents sound like they belong to this business.

?
QMS is not optional: In the agentic model, QMS comes before agents — not in parallel, not after. Agents built without QMS context produce generic outputs that clients reject. QMS is the difference between "an AI did this" and "my business did this." It is the foundation agents build on.

QMS Build Sequence
ORDER
QMS Must Be Built In This Order

The sequence matters. Each layer builds on the previous:

  • Day 1–2: Brand & Identity — This grounds everything else. Agents need to know what the client sounds like before they can write anything.
  • Day 2–3: Dictionary + Ontology — The canonical vocabulary and entity model. Agents need to know what words mean in this business context.
  • Day 4: Personas — Who the agents interact with, how they communicate, who escalates to whom.
  • Day 5: Advisors + Templates — Higher-level context that builds on the foundation. Advisors use the dictionary and ontology. Templates use the brand.

Total duration: 1 week (5 business days). Output: QMS instantiated and published to the client Notebook.

?
QMS as a living system: QMS is not "set and forget." As agents are deployed and workflows are activated, the Dictionary and Ontology evolve. New terms are added. New personas emerge. The QMS compounds knowledge over time — every sprint refines it. This is why it must exist before agents, not after.
Stage 05 · Ongoing

Continuous Agent Delivery

Agents are built in 2-week sprints. Each sprint: design 1 agent ? build ? test ? deploy. The build queue is prioritized by outcome impact. The system learns incrementally. This stage never ends — continuous delivery is the operating model.

?
Continuous delivery mindset: There is no "go-live day" where all agents deploy at once. Instead, agents are deployed one at a time, every 2 weeks, as soon as they pass testing. The system is always live. The system is always improving. Outcomes compound as more agents activate.
Sprint Cadence — 2-Week Cycles
Week 1 · Sprint N
Design & Build
  • Select next agent from outcome queue
  • Define trigger, inputs, outputs, tools
  • Build in Copilot Studio or AI Foundry
  • Wire to QMS grounding docs
  • Internal testing (5 test cases minimum)
Week 2 · Sprint N
Test & Deploy
  • Client UAT (3 real-world test cases)
  • Refinement based on UAT feedback
  • Deploy to Production environment
  • Monitor for 48 hours (hypercare)
  • Sprint review with client stakeholders
Week 1 · Sprint N+1
Monitor & Iterate
  • Previous agent now in steady state
  • Outcome metrics tracked daily
  • Next agent selected from queue
  • Build queue re-prioritized based on learnings
  • Repeat cycle
Build Queue Prioritization
QUEUE
How to Prioritize the Next Agent

At the start of each sprint, the build queue is re-evaluated. Use this decision tree:

  • Priority 1: Blocked outcomes — If an outcome is not progressing because a specific agent is missing, build that agent next.
  • Priority 2: Highest outcome impact — Which agent, if deployed, moves the needle most on a committed outcome?
  • Priority 3: Workflow enablement — Which agent is needed to complete the pilot workflow end-to-end?
  • Priority 4: Dependency resolution — Which agent is a prerequisite for 2+ other agents (e.g., a classifier that feeds multiple downstream Genies)?
  • Priority 5: Quick wins — If all else is equal, build the simplest agent (prove momentum, build confidence)

The build queue is visible to the client. The client can request re-prioritization. But the final call is NEIT's — we optimize for outcome delivery, not feature requests.

Weekly Stakeholder Check-In
WEEKLY
Sprint Review Cadence

Every Friday, 30 minutes, standing agenda:

  • Deployed this sprint: Which agent went live? What does it do? Show it working on a real Fact.
  • Outcome tracking: Current state vs. target for each committed outcome. Are we on track?
  • Next sprint preview: Which agent is queued next? Why? When will it be ready?
  • Blockers & dependencies: Anything preventing progress? Data access, stakeholder availability, integration issues?
  • Build queue adjustment: Client can request re-prioritization. NEIT confirms or explains why not.

Attendees: Client sponsor or delegate, NEIT CSM, NEIT Tech Lead. Operations lead joins when their domain is impacted.

When to Build vs. Refine
DECISION
Build New Agent vs. Improve Existing Agent

Not every sprint builds a new agent. Sometimes the best use of a sprint is refining an existing agent. Use this logic:

  • Build new if: An outcome is blocked and no existing agent can deliver it. Or the pilot workflow has a missing step.
  • Refine existing if: An agent's error rate is >5%. Or escalation rate is >25% after 4 weeks. Or client feedback says "this agent doesn't understand our business."

Refinement sprints are not wasted sprints. An agent that goes from 70% accuracy to 95% accuracy often delivers more outcome value than deploying a new 80% accurate agent.

?
Continuous delivery compounds: Sprint 1 deploys 1 agent. Sprint 2 deploys agent 2, which uses agent 1's output. Sprint 3 refines agent 1 based on what agent 2 revealed. Sprint 4 deploys agent 3, which orchestrates 1 and 2. By sprint 10, the system is doing things no individual agent could do alone. This is the compounding effect.
Stage 06 · Proof Point

Workflow Activation

The first Growth or Fulfillment workflow is the proof point — not the end state. When the workflow runs end-to-end with <15% human intervention for 2 consecutive weeks, the agentic model is validated. Then we expand to the next workflow.

?
Workflow as proof: A single deployed agent proves the agent works. A workflow proves the *system* works — that agents can orchestrate, that the QMS grounds them correctly, that outcomes compound. The workflow is where RTBoss transitions from "automation" to "operating system."
Workflow Readiness Criteria
READY
When Is a Workflow Ready to Activate?

A workflow can be activated when ALL of these are true:

  • All workflow steps have an agent: Every step in the workflow (capture ? classify ? route ? action ? verify) has a deployed Genie or Advisor assigned.
  • Agent error rate <5%: Each agent in the workflow is processing Facts with <5% error rate in standalone testing.
  • End-to-end test passed: A real Fact has been manually injected and successfully flowed through all workflow steps without human intervention.
  • Escalation paths verified: When an agent can't proceed, it escalates correctly to the right Persona.
  • Digital Twin logging verified: Every agent decision in the workflow writes to Cosmos DB.

Do not activate a workflow if any of these are false. Fix the gaps first.

Workflow Certification
CERT
How to Certify a Workflow

A workflow is certified when it meets this standard for 2 consecutive weeks:

  • <15% human intervention: Of all Facts that enter the workflow, 85%+ complete end-to-end without a human touching them. Measured via Digital Twin logs.
  • <3% error rate: Of all Facts processed, <3% result in an error (wrong classification, incorrect action, system failure).
  • No critical failures: No data loss, no compliance violations, no client-facing errors that require apology or remediation.

When certified:

  • Issue a Workflow Certificate (document stored in client SharePoint + QMS)
  • Trigger outcome bonus if the workflow delivered a contracted outcome
  • Select the next workflow from the backlog to activate
  • Celebrate with the client — this is the proof that the agentic model works
Expansion Path
EXPAND
From First Workflow to Full RTBoss

Once the first workflow is certified, the expansion path is clear:

  • Workflow 2: Select the next highest-value workflow from Discovery. Build the required agents in sprints. Activate when ready. Certify.
  • Workflow 3: Repeat. By the third workflow, many agents are reusable — the build velocity increases.
  • Cross-workflow orchestration: Once 3+ workflows are live, activate a Value Engine that orchestrates them (e.g., Growth Engine coordinates Lead Workflow + Upsell Workflow + Retention Workflow).
  • Outcome compounding: Each new workflow delivers additional outcomes. The system doesn't just automate — it optimizes across workflows.

This is how RTBoss scales. Not by deploying 50 agents at once. By proving one workflow, then the next, then the next. Each workflow increases autonomy. Each workflow reduces human dependency. The system compounds.

?
The agentic delivery model at scale: Start with outcomes. Build QMS first. Deploy agents iteratively. Prove the first workflow. Expand to the next. Repeat. Every client follows this model — Growth, Fulfillment, Innovation, Logistics. The playbook is universal. The outcomes are specific. The system compounds.
